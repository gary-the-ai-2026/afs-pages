Strategic Implication

Lock in the design moment.

This is the customer the white-glove strategy is built for. Space is their #1 barrier, project framing dominates their language, and they're already willing to pay for quality. A guided design consultation — layout, phased spec, fit guarantee — converts directly against their two largest frictions (space confusion + spousal approval) and amplifies their existing motivations (quality fixation, future-proofing).

Make the consultant credible at spec depth.

This customer doesn't need design — they need a peer. Sales consultants need to talk weight ratings, attachment compatibility, and progressive overload trajectories like a coach, not a salesperson. The "Designer" framing pivots here to "Trainer" — same white-glove, different vocabulary. Phase 2 is huge: 21% are already expanding an existing setup, meaning the door is wide open for repeat purchase if we earn their trust on call one.

Strategic Implication

Don't try to convert them into Builders — design a different funnel.

The bubble-chart truth is that this segment is loss-making at full white-glove cost-to-serve. The right move is segmentation, not conversion: offer self-serve tooling (configurator, freight calculator, finance prequal) before phone, and reserve the human consultant time for the upper two archetypes. A subset will up-tier into Home Gym Builder over 18–24 months as their finances and ambition grow — make sure the door stays open with a saved design and Phase-2 nurture, but don't subsidise the call today.

04 · The Strategic Lens

How the three archetypes map to the white-glove design strategy.

The strategy doesn't treat them identically. It treats them with the right level of human intervention — concentrated on the customer who pays for it, automated for the customer who doesn't, and pivoted in tone for the customer who wants a peer instead of a guide.

Where each archetype goes next

Three lanes, one operating model.

Home Gym Builder → Full white-glove design consult on call. Saved Design Portal record. Phase 2 nurture at 60–90 days. Fit guarantee front-and-centre. This is where "# of Home Gyms Designed" is earned.

Performance Optimiser → Same Designer Portal, but the consultant is positioned as a peer/coach. Talk progressive overload, attachment compatibility, weight ratings. Less layout, more spec. Phase 2 = the next attachment, not the next room.

Budget-Conscious Seeker → Self-serve configurator. Freight calculator before checkout. Finance prequal up front. No human consultant on inbound — reserve that capacity for the upper tiers. Saved design + nurture in case they up-tier.

05 · The One Sentence

The Home Gym Builder is the customer worth designing for. Everyone else gets the version of design they can afford to be served.

Three archetypes. One funnel. Different doses of human time. The strategy works because it stops trying to serve everyone equally — and starts serving each customer at the level the unit economics support.
